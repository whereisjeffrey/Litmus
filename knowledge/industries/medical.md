# Medical/Healthcare Evaluation Playbook

## Trust & Compliance

### Is HIPAA compliance clearly communicated?
**Tag:** [CEO]
**What to check:** HIPAA badge, privacy practices notice, data handling policy for patient information
**Why it matters:** HIPAA violations cost $100-$50,000 per incident. Patients won't share health data without trust.
**Research:** HHS: HIPAA penalties range from $100-$50,000 per violation, up to $1.5M per category per year.

### Are provider credentials and certifications displayed?
**Tag:** [CEO]
**What to check:** Doctor qualifications, board certifications, medical school, years of experience
**Why it matters:** Patients research providers extensively. Missing credentials lose patients to competitors who display them.
**Research:** Software Advice: 88% of patients consider provider credentials when choosing healthcare.

### Is there a secure patient portal?
**Tag:** [CEO]
**What to check:** Patient login, secure messaging, health records access, encrypted communication
**Why it matters:** 60% of patients prefer digital communication with providers. No portal = outdated practice.
**Research:** NRC Health: 60% of patients will switch providers for a better digital experience.

### Are patient reviews/testimonials displayed?
**Tag:** [CEO]
**What to check:** Review section, star ratings, patient stories (HIPAA-compliant)
**Why it matters:** 88% of patients use online reviews when choosing a provider.
**Research:** Software Advice: 88% consider online reviews. 72% use reviews as the first step in finding a new doctor.

## Accessibility (Critical for Healthcare)

### Does the site meet WCAG AA standards?
**Tag:** [CEO]
**What to check:** Color contrast, alt text, keyboard navigation, screen reader compatibility
**Why it matters:** Healthcare patients include elderly, visually impaired, and disabled users at higher rates than any other industry.
**Research:** CDC: 26% of US adults have a disability. Healthcare sites serve the most vulnerable populations.

### Is font size adequate for older users?
**Tag:** [CEO]
**What to check:** Base font size (16px+ minimum), line height, readability
**Why it matters:** The average age of healthcare consumers skews older. Small text = patients who can't read your site.
**Research:** AARP: adults 65+ represent the highest per-capita healthcare spending. Most need 16px+ to read comfortably.

### Is the site usable without a mouse?
**Tag:** [ENG]
**What to check:** Tab navigation, focus indicators, keyboard-accessible forms and menus
**Why it matters:** Motor impairments are common in patient populations.
**Research:** WebAIM: keyboard accessibility is foundational for users with motor disabilities.

### Are forms and error messages clear?
**Tag:** [ENG]
**What to check:** Form labels, clear error messages, required field indicators
**Why it matters:** Health-related forms are anxiety-inducing. Confusing forms cause patients to give up.
**Research:** NN/g: clear form design reduces errors by 22% and completion time by 31%.

## Appointment Booking

### Is online booking available?
**Tag:** [CEO]
**What to check:** Self-service appointment scheduling, calendar integration
**Why it matters:** Online scheduling increases booking rates by 24%. No online booking = phone tag.
**Research:** Accenture: 77% of patients want the ability to book online. Online scheduling increases bookings by 24%.

### Can you see availability without creating an account?
**Tag:** [CEO]
**What to check:** Available time slots visible before requiring patient registration
**Why it matters:** Forcing account creation before showing availability causes immediate abandonment.
**Research:** Baymard Institute principles apply: gating information behind signup reduces engagement by 30%+.

### How many steps to book?
**Tag:** [CEO]
**What to check:** Booking flow step count, required information before confirming
**Why it matters:** Every additional step loses 10-15% of potential bookings.
**Research:** Zocdoc: simplified booking flows increase completion rates by 40%.

### Is there appointment confirmation and reminders?
**Tag:** [ENG]
**What to check:** Email/SMS confirmation, automated reminders
**Why it matters:** No-show rates drop significantly with automated reminders.
**Research:** Health Management Technology: automated reminders reduce no-shows by 36%.

## Patient Communication

### Is contact information easy to find?
**Tag:** [CEO]
**What to check:** Phone number, address, email visible on every page, click-to-call on mobile
**Why it matters:** Healthcare is time-sensitive. If patients can't reach you quickly, they go to the ER or a competitor.
**Research:** Google: 60% of mobile health searches result in a phone call. Click-to-call is essential.

### Is there a telehealth option?
**Tag:** [CEO]
**What to check:** Virtual visit availability, video consultation option
**Why it matters:** Post-COVID, telehealth is expected. Practices without it lose patients who prefer virtual care.
**Research:** McKinsey: telehealth utilization stabilized at 38x pre-pandemic levels. 40% of patients prefer virtual for follow-ups.

### Are emergency procedures clear?
**Tag:** [CEO]
**What to check:** Emergency contact info, after-hours procedures, when to call 911 vs when to call the office
**Why it matters:** Medical emergencies need clear guidance. Ambiguity can have life-threatening consequences.
**Research:** Patient safety best practice: clear emergency procedures are a Joint Commission requirement.

## Provider Profiles

### Do provider profiles include photos?
**Tag:** [CEO]
**What to check:** Professional headshots, friendly appearance, consistent style
**Why it matters:** Patients want to "meet" their doctor before the visit. Photos build comfort.
**Research:** Healthgrades: provider profiles with photos get 30% more patient engagement.

### Are specialties and conditions treated listed?
**Tag:** [ENG]
**What to check:** Detailed specialty information, conditions treated, procedures performed
**Why it matters:** Patients search for specific conditions. Detailed profiles match patients to the right provider.
**Research:** Google: 77% of patients use search engines before booking. Specific content improves search visibility.

### Are accepted insurance plans listed?
**Tag:** [CEO]
**What to check:** Insurance plan list, in-network/out-of-network status
**Why it matters:** Insurance compatibility is a top-3 factor in provider selection. Missing this info loses patients immediately.
**Research:** Kaiser Family Foundation: insurance coverage is the #2 factor (after location) in choosing a provider.

## Insurance & Billing

### Is insurance information clear and accessible?
**Tag:** [CEO]
**What to check:** Accepted plans, verification process, out-of-pocket estimates
**Why it matters:** Billing confusion is the #1 source of patient complaints. Clarity reduces billing disputes by 40%.
**Research:** InstaMed: 78% of consumers are confused by medical bills. Clear upfront info reduces disputes.

### Are payment options clearly displayed?
**Tag:** [ENG]
**What to check:** Payment methods accepted, payment plans, financial assistance info
**Why it matters:** 56% of patients are surprised by medical bills. Upfront payment info builds trust.
**Research:** Waystar: transparent pricing reduces billing complaints by 40%.

### Is there a patient financial assistance policy?
**Tag:** [ENG]
**What to check:** Charity care info, sliding scale fees, financial assistance application
**Why it matters:** Community benefit reporting and ACA requirements may mandate this disclosure.
**Research:** ACA Section 501(r): nonprofit hospitals must have a financial assistance policy. Good practice for all providers.

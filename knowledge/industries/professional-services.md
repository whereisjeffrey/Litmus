# Professional Services Evaluation Playbook
## (Law Firms, Accounting, Consulting, Agencies)

## Credibility & Trust

### Does the website look professional and modern?
**Tag:** [CEO]
**What to check:** Visual design quality, modern typography, professional photography vs stock
**Why it matters:** 75% of people judge a firm's credibility based on website design alone. An outdated site signals an outdated firm.
**Research:** Stanford Web Credibility Project: 75% judge credibility by design. First impressions form in 50ms (Lindgaard et al., 2006).

### Are partner/team credentials prominently displayed?
**Tag:** [CEO]
**What to check:** Professional bios, education, certifications, bar admissions, CPA licenses
**Why it matters:** Professional services sell expertise. If credentials aren't visible, prospects can't verify competence.
**Research:** Hinge Marketing: 80% of professional service buyers check credentials before making contact.

### Are case studies or client results featured?
**Tag:** [CEO]
**What to check:** Case study section with specific outcomes, industry/matter types, quantified results
**Why it matters:** Case studies are the most effective content for professional service firms — they prove you can deliver.
**Research:** Content Marketing Institute: case studies generate 70% more leads for professional services firms.

### Are client testimonials specific and attributed?
**Tag:** [CEO]
**What to check:** Named testimonials with titles, companies, specific outcomes
**Why it matters:** "Great law firm" is meaningless. "Saved us $2M in litigation costs — Jane Smith, CFO" is convincing.
**Research:** BrightLocal: testimonials with specific details are trusted 58% more than vague praise.

### Are industry awards and recognition displayed?
**Tag:** [ENG]
**What to check:** Best Lawyers, Super Lawyers, Inc. 5000, industry-specific awards
**Why it matters:** Third-party recognition builds credibility beyond self-promotion.
**Research:** Hinge: award mentions increase perceived expertise by 34%.

## Consultation Booking

### Can prospects book a consultation online?
**Tag:** [CEO]
**What to check:** Online scheduling, Calendly embed, "Book a Consultation" CTA
**Why it matters:** Online booking increases intake conversion by 30%. Phone-only booking loses digital-first prospects.
**Research:** Clio Legal Trends Report: online booking increases intake conversion by 30%.

### Is the initial consultation free or fee-based (and is it clear)?
**Tag:** [CEO]
**What to check:** Consultation pricing visibility, "Free consultation" or fee amount displayed
**Why it matters:** Hidden consultation fees surprise and alienate prospects. Clarity builds trust.
**Research:** FindLaw: 72% of potential legal clients expect to know consultation costs before calling.

### Is the contact form simple?
**Tag:** [CEO]
**What to check:** Form field count (benchmark: 4-5 for professional services)
**Why it matters:** Long intake forms deter initial contact. Get the conversation started, ask details later.
**Research:** HubSpot: reducing form fields increases submission rates significantly. Each field removed = ~10% more leads.

### Is the response time expectation set?
**Tag:** [ENG]
**What to check:** "We'll respond within 24 hours" or similar response time commitment
**Why it matters:** No response time expectation = anxiety. Prospects may contact competitors while waiting.
**Research:** InsideSales: responding within 5 minutes is 100x more effective than 30 minutes.

## Service Descriptions

### Are practice areas/services clearly described?
**Tag:** [CEO]
**What to check:** Individual service pages, plain-language descriptions, scope of work explained
**Why it matters:** Prospects need to know if you handle their specific problem. Vague descriptions lose qualified leads.
**Research:** Hinge: firms with detailed service descriptions generate 40% more qualified leads.

### Is industry specialization communicated?
**Tag:** [CEO]
**What to check:** Industry focus areas, vertical expertise, sector-specific experience
**Why it matters:** Clients prefer specialists over generalists. "We serve everyone" means you're expert at nothing.
**Research:** Hinge: specialized firms grow 8x faster than generalist firms.

### Is the process explained for new clients?
**Tag:** [CEO]
**What to check:** "What to expect" section, engagement process, timeline information
**Why it matters:** Professional services are opaque to most buyers. Explaining the process reduces anxiety and increases conversion.
**Research:** Clio: 68% of legal consumers want to understand the process before hiring.

## Pricing

### Is there any pricing transparency?
**Tag:** [CEO]
**What to check:** Fee ranges, pricing models (hourly, flat fee, contingency), starting-at prices
**Why it matters:** Professional services are notoriously opaque on pricing. Any transparency is a competitive advantage.
**Research:** Clio Legal Trends: 80% of legal consumers want pricing information before making contact.

### Are billing models explained?
**Tag:** [ENG]
**What to check:** How billing works, retainer explanations, expense pass-through policies
**Why it matters:** Billing surprises destroy client relationships. Clear billing models set expectations.
**Research:** LexisNexis: billing disputes are the #1 cause of client dissatisfaction in professional services.

## Client Results & Social Proof

### Are results quantified where possible?
**Tag:** [CEO]
**What to check:** Specific metrics in case studies — dollars saved, cases won, audit findings reduced
**Why it matters:** "We helped a client" is weak. "We saved a Fortune 500 client $4.2M in tax liability" is compelling.
**Research:** CMI: quantified results in case studies increase perceived value by 65%.

### Is there a client logo wall?
**Tag:** [CEO]
**What to check:** Client/brand logos displayed (with permission)
**Why it matters:** Recognizable clients create instant credibility association.
**Research:** Basecamp: client logos increase conversion 8.9%. Effect is amplified in B2B professional services.

### Are reviews on third-party platforms?
**Tag:** [ENG]
**What to check:** Google Reviews, Avvo, Clutch, G2 ratings linked or embedded
**Why it matters:** Self-hosted testimonials are less trusted than independent reviews.
**Research:** BrightLocal: 87% of consumers read online reviews for local businesses (including professional services).

## Content & Expertise

### Is there a blog or thought leadership section?
**Tag:** [CEO]
**What to check:** Regular content publishing, article quality, relevance to target clients
**Why it matters:** Content demonstrates expertise and drives organic traffic. It's how 70% of clients find professional service firms.
**Research:** Hinge: professional service firms with active blogs generate 70% more leads.

### Is content written for clients, not peers?
**Tag:** [CEO]
**What to check:** Reading level, jargon usage, client-centric vs practitioner-centric language
**Why it matters:** Legal/accounting jargon impresses other lawyers/accountants. It confuses and alienates clients.
**Research:** NN/g: content at an 8th-grade reading level reaches the widest audience and highest engagement.

### Is there a resource library or knowledge base?
**Tag:** [ENG]
**What to check:** Downloadable guides, FAQs, educational resources, webinar recordings
**Why it matters:** Educational content nurtures prospects who aren't ready to engage yet.
**Research:** Demand Gen Report: 47% of B2B buyers consume 3-5 pieces of content before engaging with sales.

## Mobile & Technical

### Is the site mobile-optimized?
**Tag:** [CEO]
**What to check:** Responsive design, mobile navigation, click-to-call, mobile-friendly forms
**Why it matters:** 60% of professional service searches happen on mobile. A broken mobile experience loses over half your prospects.
**Research:** Google: 60%+ of searches are mobile. Sites not mobile-optimized lose 61% of mobile visitors.

### Does the site load in under 3 seconds?
**Tag:** [ENG]
**What to check:** Page load time, image optimization, script loading
**Why it matters:** Slow sites feel unprofessional. Speed is a trust signal.
**Research:** Google: 53% of mobile visitors abandon sites loading over 3 seconds.

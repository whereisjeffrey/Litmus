# Landing Page Evaluation Playbook

## Message Clarity

### Can you understand what they offer in 5 seconds?
**Tag:** [CEO]
**What to check:** Headline specificity, value proposition clarity, product/service comprehension
**Why it matters:** Visitors decide to stay or leave in seconds. If they can't understand what you do, you've lost them forever.
**Research:** 55% of visitors spend less than 15 seconds on a page (Microsoft, 2015). Chartbeat: most visitors who bounce do so within 5 seconds.

### Does the headline address a specific pain point?
**Tag:** [CEO]
**What to check:** Problem-focused vs feature-focused headline language
**Why it matters:** Pain-driven headlines create emotional connection. Feature headlines are forgettable.
**Research:** Copyblogger: 80% read the headline, only 20% read the rest. The headline must earn attention.

### Is the target audience immediately clear?
**Tag:** [CEO]
**What to check:** Language that identifies who this is for — persona, industry, role
**Why it matters:** When everyone is the target, no one feels targeted. Specificity increases conversion.
**Research:** HubSpot: personalized CTAs convert 202% better than generic ones.

### Is there a clear subheadline that explains how?
**Tag:** [ENG]
**What to check:** Supporting text under headline that explains the mechanism/approach
**Why it matters:** Headline = what/why, subheadline = how. Both are needed for comprehension.
**Research:** NN/g: users scan in F-patterns. Headline + subheadline capture the most attention.

## Persuasion Structure

### Does the page follow Problem → Solution → Proof → CTA?
**Tag:** [CEO]
**What to check:** Content flow structure — does it build a logical case?
**Why it matters:** Unstructured pages feel like brochures. Structured pages feel like conversations that lead to action.
**Research:** Unbounce: landing pages with clear narrative structure convert 30% better than feature-list pages.

### Are objections addressed on the page?
**Tag:** [CEO]
**What to check:** FAQ section, objection-handling content, risk-reduction language
**Why it matters:** Unaddressed objections are silent conversion killers — the visitor leaves with their doubt.
**Research:** MarketingSherpa: addressing the top 3 objections on-page can increase conversion by 80%.

### Is there urgency without being manipulative?
**Tag:** [CEO]
**What to check:** Limited-time offers, countdown timers, scarcity indicators (must be truthful)
**Why it matters:** Legitimate urgency motivates action. Fake urgency destroys trust.
**Research:** CXL: real urgency/scarcity increases conversion 9-15%. Fake urgency damages brand trust.

## CTA Design

### Is there ONE clear primary CTA above the fold?
**Tag:** [CEO]
**What to check:** Number of CTAs visible without scrolling, visual hierarchy
**Why it matters:** Multiple competing CTAs cause decision paralysis. One clear action converts best.
**Research:** Unbounce: pages with one CTA convert at 13.5% vs 2.8% for pages with 5+ CTAs.

### Does the CTA text describe a specific action?
**Tag:** [CEO]
**What to check:** Button text — "Start Free Trial" vs "Submit", "Get Your Report" vs "Click Here"
**Why it matters:** Specific CTAs set expectations and reduce anxiety about what happens next.
**Research:** HubSpot: personalized, specific CTAs outperform generic ones by 202%.

### Does the CTA stand out visually?
**Tag:** [ENG]
**What to check:** Button contrast against background, size, whitespace, visual weight
**Why it matters:** If the CTA doesn't visually pop, visitors don't know where to click.
**Research:** Eyetracking studies (NN/g): high-contrast CTAs receive 3x more fixations.

### Is the CTA repeated after key sections?
**Tag:** [ENG]
**What to check:** CTA buttons placed after social proof, features, and testimonials sections
**Why it matters:** Different visitors are convinced at different points — give them the action when they're ready.
**Research:** ContentVerve: placing CTAs at multiple decision points increased conversions by 20%.

## Lead Capture

### How many form fields for lead gen?
**Tag:** [CEO]
**What to check:** Field count on lead capture form (benchmark: 3-5 max)
**Why it matters:** Every field is a micro-barrier. The more you ask for, the fewer leads you get.
**Research:** HubSpot: reducing form fields from 4 to 3 increases conversion by 50%.

### Is the value exchange clear?
**Tag:** [CEO]
**What to check:** What the visitor gets in return for their email — is it specific and compelling?
**Why it matters:** "Sign up for updates" converts at 1%. "Get the free 50-page playbook" converts at 15%.
**Research:** Sumo: clear value propositions in lead magnets increase opt-in rates 5-10x over generic newsletter signups.

### Is privacy addressed near the form?
**Tag:** [ENG]
**What to check:** Privacy policy link, "we won't spam you" text near submit
**Why it matters:** Privacy assurance reduces form anxiety.
**Research:** ContentVerve: adding "We will never share your email" near the form increased signups by 19%.

## Social Proof

### Are there testimonials with specific results?
**Tag:** [CEO]
**What to check:** Customer quotes with measurable outcomes, not vague praise
**Why it matters:** "Increased revenue by 47%" is credible. "Great product!" is noise.
**Research:** BrightLocal (2023): 49% trust specific reviews as much as personal recommendations.

### Are customer logos displayed prominently?
**Tag:** [CEO]
**What to check:** Recognizable brand logos near the hero section
**Why it matters:** Brand association creates instant credibility — "if Google uses it, it must be good."
**Research:** Basecamp: adding customer logos increased signups by 8.9%.

### Is there a user/customer count or social metric?
**Tag:** [CEO]
**What to check:** "Join 50,000+ teams" or similar social proof numbers
**Why it matters:** Large numbers create bandwagon effect and reduce perceived risk.
**Research:** Cialdini (Influence): social proof is one of the 6 principles of persuasion. Specific numbers outperform vague claims.

### Are there third-party review badges?
**Tag:** [ENG]
**What to check:** G2, Capterra, Trustpilot ratings displayed
**Why it matters:** Third-party validation is more trusted than self-promotion.
**Research:** G2: 92% of B2B buyers are more likely to purchase after reading a trusted review.

## Above the Fold

### Is there a hero image or product screenshot?
**Tag:** [CEO]
**What to check:** Visual content above fold — stock photo vs actual product
**Why it matters:** Stock photos signal "we have nothing to show." Product screenshots build confidence.
**Research:** VWO: replacing stock photos with product screenshots increased signups 35%.

### Is the hero carousel-free?
**Tag:** [CEO]
**What to check:** Static hero vs rotating carousel/slider
**Why it matters:** Carousels dilute the message — only the first slide gets real attention.
**Research:** NN/g: auto-rotating carousels are often ignored. First slide gets 89% of clicks. University of Notre Dame: only 1% interact with carousels.

### Is white space used effectively?
**Tag:** [ENG]
**What to check:** Spacing around headline, CTA, and key elements
**Why it matters:** Crowded above-fold sections feel overwhelming and reduce comprehension.
**Research:** Wichita State University: white space increases comprehension by 20%.

## Content Flow

### Are features described as benefits?
**Tag:** [CEO]
**What to check:** Feature descriptions — "AI-powered analytics" vs "Save 10 hours/week on reporting"
**Why it matters:** Buyers don't buy features, they buy outcomes. Benefits sell, features don't.
**Research:** Copyhackers: benefit-driven copy outperforms feature-driven copy by 28%.

### Is there no wall of text?
**Tag:** [ENG]
**What to check:** Content density, paragraph length, use of bullets, headers, and visuals
**Why it matters:** Walls of text are never read. Scannable content is consumed 2.5x more.
**Research:** NN/g: users read at most 28% of words. Scannable content with bullets and headers improves usability by 47%.

### Is the page mobile-optimized?
**Tag:** [CEO]
**What to check:** Responsive layout, mobile CTA placement, readable text without zoom
**Why it matters:** 60%+ of traffic is mobile. A broken mobile experience loses more than half your visitors.
**Research:** Statista (2024): mobile generates 60.67% of global web traffic.

# SaaS Evaluation Playbook

## First Impression & Value Proposition

### Can visitors understand what you do in 5 seconds?
**Tag:** [CEO]
**What to check:** Headline clarity, specificity of value proposition, presence of product screenshot
**Why it matters:** If visitors can't instantly grasp your product, they leave — you're paying for traffic that never converts.
**Research:** 55% of visitors spend less than 15 seconds on a page (Microsoft, 2015). Unbounce found landing pages with clear, specific headlines convert 30% better than vague ones.

### Is the hero section a stock photo or your actual product?
**Tag:** [CEO]
**What to check:** Hero image content — stock photography vs product screenshot/demo
**Why it matters:** Visitors want to see the product before committing — stock photos signal "we have nothing to show."
**Research:** VWO case study: replacing stock photo with product screenshot increased signups 35%.

### Does the headline address a pain point or just describe a feature?
**Tag:** [CEO]
**What to check:** Headline framing — "Save 10 hours/week on reporting" vs "Analytics Dashboard Tool"
**Why it matters:** Pain-driven headlines connect emotionally; feature-driven headlines are forgettable.
**Research:** Copyblogger research: 8 out of 10 people read the headline, only 2 read the rest. The headline must earn their attention.

### Is there a clear single CTA above the fold?
**Tag:** [CEO]
**What to check:** Number of CTAs visible without scrolling, visual prominence
**Why it matters:** Multiple competing CTAs reduce conversion — visitors freeze when given too many choices.
**Research:** Unbounce: pages with a single CTA convert 13.5% vs 2.8% for pages with 5+ CTAs.

### Does the page look modern and professional?
**Tag:** [CEO]
**What to check:** Visual design quality, typography, spacing, color usage
**Why it matters:** 75% of users judge credibility based on visual design alone.
**Research:** Stanford Web Credibility Project (2002, validated in follow-ups): visual design is the #1 factor in website credibility judgments.

## Pricing & Packaging

### Is the recommended plan visually obvious?
**Tag:** [CEO]
**What to check:** Highlighted/featured plan, "Most Popular" badge, visual differentiation
**Why it matters:** Without guidance, visitors pick the cheapest option or leave confused.
**Research:** ConversionXL (2019): highlighting a recommended plan increases selection rate by 20%.

### Are there too many pricing tiers?
**Tag:** [CEO]
**What to check:** Number of visible plans (ideal: 3-4)
**Why it matters:** More than 4 plans causes choice paralysis — visitors can't compare and leave.
**Research:** Hick's Law + Columbia "jam study" (Iyengar & Lepper, 2000): reducing choices from 24 to 6 increased purchase rate 10x.

### Is there an annual/monthly toggle with savings shown?
**Tag:** [CEO]
**What to check:** Billing frequency toggle, savings percentage/amount displayed
**Why it matters:** Annual plans improve cash flow and reduce churn. Showing savings drives annual selection.
**Research:** ProfitWell: companies offering annual plans with visible savings see 15-20% higher annual plan selection.

### Is pricing hidden behind "Contact Sales"?
**Tag:** [CEO]
**What to check:** Whether prices are visible or gated behind a sales contact
**Why it matters:** For SMB-focused products, hidden pricing drives away 70%+ of prospects who want self-service.
**Research:** OpenView Partners: SaaS companies with transparent pricing grow 2x faster than those that hide prices.

### Is there a free tier or trial clearly shown?
**Tag:** [CEO]
**What to check:** Free plan or trial offer visibility and prominence
**Why it matters:** Free trials reduce risk perception — the #1 barrier to SaaS adoption.
**Research:** Totango: average SaaS free trial conversion rate is 15-25%. No trial = no top-of-funnel.

### Can features be compared across plans easily?
**Tag:** [ENG]
**What to check:** Feature comparison table, checkmarks, clear differentiation
**Why it matters:** Users need to understand what they get at each tier to make a confident decision.
**Research:** Baymard Institute: 67% of users compare plans side-by-side before purchasing.

## Signup & Onboarding

### How many fields before trying the product?
**Tag:** [CEO]
**What to check:** Field count on signup form (benchmark: 3 or fewer)
**Why it matters:** Every additional field loses 10% of potential signups. The gap between 3 fields and 9 fields is enormous.
**Research:** Imagescape study: each form field removed increases conversion by ~10%. Expedia removed one field and gained $12M/year.

### Is social login available?
**Tag:** [CEO]
**What to check:** Google, Apple, GitHub, or Microsoft sign-in options
**Why it matters:** Social login reduces friction to near-zero — one click vs filling out a form.
**Research:** LoginRadius: social login can increase signups by 20-40% depending on audience.

### Does signup deliver immediate value?
**Tag:** [CEO]
**What to check:** What happens after signup — empty dashboard vs guided experience
**Why it matters:** If users see a blank screen, 40-60% never come back.
**Research:** Userpilot: onboarding completion rates average 40-60%. Products with guided first experiences retain 2.5x more users.

### Is there a guided onboarding flow?
**Tag:** [CEO]
**What to check:** Product tour, setup wizard, checklist, or progressive disclosure
**Why it matters:** Users who complete onboarding are 3x more likely to convert from trial to paid.
**Research:** Appcues: users who complete onboarding retain at 80% vs 25% for those who don't.

### Does the CTA say something specific?
**Tag:** [ENG]
**What to check:** Button text — "Start Free Trial" vs "Submit" or "Sign Up"
**Why it matters:** Specific CTAs outperform generic ones significantly.
**Research:** HubSpot: personalized CTAs convert 202% better than default "Submit" buttons.

### Are password requirements shown upfront?
**Tag:** [ENG]
**What to check:** Password criteria visible before/during typing, not just on error
**Why it matters:** Surprise validation errors cause frustration and abandonment.
**Research:** Nielsen Norman Group: inline validation reduces errors by 22% and completion time by 31%.

## Sales Process

### How far out is the earliest demo slot?
**Tag:** [CEO]
**What to check:** Demo booking availability — days until first available slot
**Why it matters:** Every day between "I'm interested" and "I see the demo" loses prospects to competitors.
**Research:** InsideSales: responding to leads within 5 minutes is 100x more effective than waiting 30 minutes.

### Is there a self-serve option alongside "Talk to Sales"?
**Tag:** [CEO]
**What to check:** Whether visitors can buy or start a trial without a sales call
**Why it matters:** 75% of B2B buyers prefer a rep-free experience (Gartner, 2023). Forcing sales calls loses self-serve buyers.
**Research:** Gartner (2023): 75% of B2B buyers prefer self-service purchasing.

### Is booking embedded or does it require email?
**Tag:** [ENG]
**What to check:** Calendly/embedded booking vs "Email us" for demos
**Why it matters:** Each friction step in booking loses 20-30% of prospects.
**Research:** Chili Piper: embedded booking converts 2x better than "email us to schedule."

## Navigation & Information Architecture

### Can you find pricing in one click from homepage?
**Tag:** [CEO]
**What to check:** Pricing link in main navigation, visible without dropdown
**Why it matters:** Pricing is the #2 most visited page on SaaS sites. Hiding it frustrates buyers.
**Research:** Price Intelligently: pricing page is 2nd most visited after homepage for SaaS sites.

### Are there too many navigation items?
**Tag:** [CEO]
**What to check:** Main nav item count (benchmark: 7 or fewer)
**Why it matters:** Cognitive overload in navigation causes visitors to bounce rather than explore.
**Research:** Miller's Law: working memory holds 7±2 items. Nielsen Norman: simplified navigation increases task completion by 30%.

### Is search available for content-heavy sites?
**Tag:** [ENG]
**What to check:** Search functionality presence and placement
**Why it matters:** 30% of site visitors use search as their primary navigation method.
**Research:** eConsultancy: site search users convert 1.8x more than non-search users.

### Is the footer a dumping ground or organized?
**Tag:** [ENG]
**What to check:** Footer organization, link grouping, relevance
**Why it matters:** 65% of users scroll to the footer for navigation when they can't find what they need.
**Research:** Chartbeat: footer engagement is significant — users who reach it are highly engaged.

## Trust & Social Proof

### Are there customer logos above the fold?
**Tag:** [CEO]
**What to check:** Recognizable brand logos in the hero section or just below
**Why it matters:** Brand logos create instant credibility — "if they trust it, I can too."
**Research:** Basecamp case study: adding customer logos increased signups by 8.9%.

### Do testimonials include specific results?
**Tag:** [CEO]
**What to check:** Testimonial content — vague praise vs specific metrics/outcomes
**Why it matters:** "Great product!" means nothing. "Reduced our reporting time by 70%" is credible.
**Research:** BrightLocal (2023): 49% of consumers trust reviews with specific details as much as personal recommendations.

### Are case studies available with real numbers?
**Tag:** [CEO]
**What to check:** Case study section with quantifiable outcomes
**Why it matters:** Case studies with metrics are the #1 most effective B2B content type.
**Research:** Content Marketing Institute: case studies are rated the most effective content type by 73% of B2B marketers.

### Is there third-party validation?
**Tag:** [CEO]
**What to check:** G2, Capterra, Trustpilot ratings, awards, press mentions
**Why it matters:** Third-party validation is more trusted than self-promotion.
**Research:** G2: 92% of B2B buyers are more likely to purchase after reading a trusted review.

## Content & Messaging

### Is copy written for the buyer or the product team?
**Tag:** [CEO]
**What to check:** Language — benefits vs features, customer-centric vs product-centric
**Why it matters:** Buyers don't care about your technology. They care about their problems being solved.
**Research:** Copyhackers: benefit-driven copy outperforms feature-driven copy by 28% in A/B tests.

### Is there too much jargon?
**Tag:** [CEO]
**What to check:** Industry/technical jargon usage, reading level
**Why it matters:** If your target buyer can't understand your page, they won't buy. Write for the decision-maker, not the engineer.
**Research:** NN/g: users read at most 28% of words on a page. Every word of jargon is a lost reader.

### Are CTAs action-oriented?
**Tag:** [ENG]
**What to check:** Button text — verbs vs nouns, specific vs generic
**Why it matters:** "Get Started Free" outperforms "Sign Up" significantly.
**Research:** HubSpot: CTAs with action verbs increase click-through by 93%.

## Technical Performance

### Does the page load in under 3 seconds?
**Tag:** [CEO]
**What to check:** Page load time, Largest Contentful Paint
**Why it matters:** Speed directly impacts revenue — every second of delay costs conversions.
**Research:** Google: 53% of mobile users abandon sites that take over 3 seconds to load. Akamai: a 1-second delay reduces conversions by 7%.

### Is there layout shift during loading?
**Tag:** [ENG]
**What to check:** Cumulative Layout Shift (CLS), elements jumping around
**Why it matters:** Layout shift is jarring and reduces trust — it makes the site feel broken.
**Research:** Google: pages with low CLS have 24% lower bounce rates.

### Are fonts loading without flash?
**Tag:** [ENG]
**What to check:** Font loading strategy, FOUT/FOIT issues
**Why it matters:** Flash of unstyled text looks unprofessional.
**Research:** Web.dev: font-display: swap eliminates invisible text during load.

### Is the site responsive across breakpoints?
**Tag:** [ENG]
**What to check:** Layout at mobile, tablet, and desktop widths
**Why it matters:** 60%+ of traffic is mobile. A broken mobile layout loses more than half your visitors.
**Research:** Statista (2024): mobile devices generate 60.67% of global website traffic.
